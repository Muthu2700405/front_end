Karaikudi is the largest city of Sivaganga district in the Indian state of Tamil Nadu. It is the centre of the Karaikudi Metropolitan Area, the 21st largest urban Agglomeration of Tamil Nadu based on 2011 census data. It is part of the area commonly referred to as Chettinad and has been declared a City Municipal Corporation by the Government of Tamil Nadu, on account of the palatial houses built with limestone called Kaarai veedu. which undergoes major developments as part of Government Schemes. This city is famous for Karaikudi Kandangi sarees a special type of sarees which come from and are woven in this area. The geographical indication presents the government of India of this Kandangi sarees. Chettinad cuisine originated in the areas around Karaikudi. It is one of the few towns and cities in Tamil Nadu selected for AMRUT Schemes from central government and the developmental activities are taken care by the government of Tamil Nadu.

Whenever we hear or come across the word Chettinad, our mind goes to the famed spicy, fiery and peppery cuisine of India’s South. However, Tamil Nadu’s Chettinad, particularly the enchanting town of Karaikudi in this region, offers much more than just its delectable dishes. Well connected by rail and road, and with amazing options like heritage houses or guesthouses to stay in, Karaikudi is a must-visit for those wanting to know India better.

Steeped in rich history, cultural heritage and architectural marvels, Karaikudi is a stunning place that is waiting to be explored by true wanderers. So join us on a journey to uncover the charm of this captivating Chettinad region.
